LiDAR vs Camera: Choosing the Right Sensors

A practical guide to selecting sensors for autonomous systems based on real-world deployment experience across diverse environments.

LiDAR: Precise depth measurement, works in darkness, immune to visual texture. Camera: Rich semantic information, color/texture recognition, lower cost. Radar: Weather resilient, velocity measurement, long range.

In port terminal operations, LiDAR excels at precise positioning for container handling, while cameras are essential for reading container IDs and detecting humans. The combination proved more robust than either alone.

While cameras are significantly cheaper, the total system cost includes processing, calibration, and maintenance. Sometimes a single high-quality LiDAR is more cost-effective than managing multiple camera systems.

Key Takeaways

  • No universal answer
  • Use case driven selection
  • Consider total system cost
  • Multi-modal is robust
  • Environmental factors
  • Safety requirements
