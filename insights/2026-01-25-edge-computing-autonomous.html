Edge Computing for Autonomous Systems

Cloud-based inference introduces latency that autonomous systems cannot afford. Moving AI to the edge isn't optional — it's a safety requirement.

An autonomous vehicle travelling at 60 km/h covers 16 metres every second. A 100ms round-trip to the cloud means decisions are made 1.6 metres too late. Edge computing eliminates this latency by processing sensor data directly on the vehicle, enabling sub-10ms inference times critical for safety.

Modern edge platforms like NVIDIA Jetson AGX Orin deliver up to 275 TOPS of AI performance in under 60W. Combined with TensorRT optimisation, models that take 50ms on a desktop GPU can run in under 8ms on these platforms. The key is designing your inference pipeline around the hardware constraints from day one.

Successful edge deployment requires model quantisation (FP32 to INT8), operator fusion, and memory-efficient data pipelines. We've achieved 6x inference speedup through systematic optimisation while maintaining 99.5% of the original model's accuracy — a trade-off that's well worth it for real-time operation.

Key Takeaways

  • Sub-10ms inference latency
  • TensorRT optimisation
  • INT8 quantisation
  • Power-efficient compute
  • On-device sensor fusion
  • OTA model updates
