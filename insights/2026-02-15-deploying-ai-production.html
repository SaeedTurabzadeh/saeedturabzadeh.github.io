Deploying AI Models in Production: Lessons from the Field

Training a deep learning model is only half the battle. Deploying it in a real-time, safety-critical system is where the real challenges emerge.

A model that runs at 100ms in the lab might not be acceptable when you need 30Hz perception. Optimization through TensorRT, quantization, and architecture pruning becomes essential.

No matter how comprehensive your training data, production will expose scenarios you never anticipated. Build monitoring systems that capture these cases for continuous improvement.

What happens when your model fails? Design fallback behaviors. In autonomous systems, this might mean switching to a simpler but more reliable algorithm or safely stopping.

Key Takeaways

  • Optimize for latency
  • Monitor edge cases
  • Design fallbacks
  • Version control all
  • Focus on reliability
  • Graceful failure handling
